A Tribute Page To:

B.J. Habibie

1936 - 2019

Prof. Dr.-Ing. H. Bacharuddin Jusuf Habibie

Brief story of "Mr. Crack"

An Indonesian engineer and politician who was the third president of Indonesia from 1998 to 1999, after succeeded Suharto who resigned after 31 years in office. His presidency is seen as a landmark and transition to the Reformation era. Upon becoming president, he liberalized Indonesia's press and political party laws.

He also known as the Father of Technology in Indonesia as he pioneered the first Indonesian made passanger airplane N-250 Gatotkaca. Habibie had also created a DO-31 aircraft design, aircraft with propeller technology that could land and take off vertically. The blueprint was later purchased by the United States Space Agency, NASA.

Habibie got the title Mr. Crack after he discovered the crack propagation theory. It was a mathematical model that useful for predicting crack propagation behavior in aircraft structures to the atomic level. This theory is important because at that time many aircraft accidents occurred due to structural failure.

If you interested, you can read more at his Wikipedia's page.

Habibie's Timeline

25 June 1936
Born at Parepare

Born from a married couple, Alwi Abdul Jalil Habibie, an agriculturist of Gorontalese descent, and R. A. Tuti Marini Puspowardojo, a Javanese noblewoman from Yogyakarta, met while studying in Bogor.

1960
Received an engineer's degree in Germany

He remained in Germany as a research assistant under Hans Ebner at the Lehrstuhl und Institut für Leichtbau, RWTH Aachen to conduct research for his doctoral degree.

1962
Married Hasri Ainun

Habibie return to Indonesia for sick leave. He was reacquainted with Hasri Ainun, a girl he known from his childhood . The two married on 12 May 1962, returning to Germany shortly afterwards.

1965 - 1974
Doctorate and Career

After he obtain doctorate in 1965, Habibie did accept a position with Messerschmitt-Bölkow-Blohm in Hamburg. There, he developed theories on thermodynamics, construction, and aerodynamics known as the Habibie Factor, Habibie Theorem, and Habibie Method, respectively.

1974 - 1998
Return to Indonesia

In 1974, President Suharto recruited Habibie to return to Indonesia as part of his drive to industrialize and develop the country. In 1995 as he already became State Minister of Research and Technology, he pioneered the first Indonesian made passenger airplane, N-250 Gatotkaca.

1998 - 1999
Vice President - President

Despite Asian Financial Crisis, Habibie was elected as Vice President in March 1998. On 21 May 1998, just two months into Habibie's vice presidency, Suharto announced his resignation. Habibie, as the Constitution says, succeeded him as president.

1999 - 2019
Retire and Final Years

After relinquishing the presidency, Habibie spent more time in Germany than in Indonesia, though he was active during Susilo Bambang Yudhoyono's presidency as a presidential adviser. During this time, he established the Habibie Centre, an independent think tank.

11 September 2019
Rest In Peace

In early September 2019, he was admitted to Gatot Soebroto Army Hospital, where he was undergoing treatments for heart problems, namely cardiomyopathy, and died on 11 September 2019. He was buried the next afternoon at the Kalibata Heroes Cemetery, next to his wife's grave.
